Beware of Phishing

Email Phishing

Phishing is an attempt to steal personal information or break into online accounts using deceptive emails, messages, ads, or sites that look similar to sites you already use.

Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing method where cybercriminals pose as a trusted source to convince you to give away confidential data, personal information, or other sensitive details.

Pharming

Pharming is a cyberattack intended to redirect a website's traffic to another, fake site by installing a malicious program on the computer.

Whaling

Targeting high-level executives such as CEOs, CFOs, and COOs the goal is to trick the executive into revealing sensitive information and corporate data. These targets are carefully selected because of their access and authority within an organization.

HTTP Phishing

Cybercriminals are often leveraging HTTPS in the links to lure victims to a malicious website that appears to be secure. It's important not to trust a website just because it has a padlock icon or HTTPS in the address bar.

Watering Hole Phishing

Watering hole phishing is when a malicious cybercriminal uses a website your company employees visit often (for example, industry news or third-party vendors websites) and infects the IP address with malicious code or downloads.

Phish-Proof Tips
Stay one step ahead of cyber tricksters with these savvy tips to outsmart phishing attempts.
  • Spot the Phish: Learn to identify suspicious emails by checking for spelling errors, unusual sender addresses, and urgent demands for personal information.
  • Think Before You Click: Hover over links to reveal their true destination before clicking, and avoid downloading attachments or providing sensitive data without verifying the source.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up with the latest phishing trends and tactics to recognize evolving threats and protect yourself from falling into the phishing net.
  • Double-Check Requests: Verify unexpected requests for personal or financial information by contacting the supposed sender through official channels to confirm the legitimacy of the communication.
  • Secure Your Accounts: Use strong, unique passwords for each online account, enable two-factor authentication whenever possible, and regularly update your security software to shield against phishing attempts.
